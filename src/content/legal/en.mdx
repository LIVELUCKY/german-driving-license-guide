## ⚠️ Disclaimer

This is an unofficial study aid. All rules and sign descriptions are simplified for learning purposes. They do not replace official sources. For binding information, consult the official Straßenverkehrs-Ordnung (StVO), the TÜV/DEKRA theory exam material, and your licensed Fahrschule. The authors accept no liability for decisions made based on the content of this site.

## 🚦 Road sign artwork

### German signs (StVO)

- **Source:** [osmberlin/osm-traffic-sign-tool](https://github.com/osmberlin/osm-traffic-sign-tool) and Wikimedia Commons
- **Licence:** German traffic signs are official government works under § 5 Abs. 1 UrhG (German Copyright Act) and are therefore in the public domain in Germany.
- **Attribution:** No attribution required, but we credit the OSM Berlin project and Wikimedia Commons contributors for their SVG renderings.

### Austrian signs (STVO AT)

- **Source:** [osmberlin/osm-traffic-sign-tool](https://github.com/osmberlin/osm-traffic-sign-tool)
- **Licence:** Austrian official signs are published by the Federal Ministry of Climate Action and are in the public domain under Austrian copyright law.

### French signs (Code de la route)

- **Source:** [osmberlin/osm-traffic-sign-tool](https://github.com/osmberlin/osm-traffic-sign-tool)
- **Licence:** French official road signs are works of the French State and free for educational use under the Légifrance open-licence framework.
